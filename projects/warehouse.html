Project Overview

Designed and implemented conveyor automation systems across three different facilities. Configured over 500 I/O points including sensors and buttons for sequential product conveying with synchronized operation. Built Modbus communication network for controlling AC drives operating 20 conveyors.

Completed end-to-end implementation including conveyor wiring, control panel design, and internal panel wiring. Achieved significant reduction in processing time and doubled workforce efficiency through automation.

Technical Approach

The conveyor automation systems were built using Fatek PLCs and HMIs for reliable control and operator interface. Veichi AC drives controlled 20 conveyor motors with speed regulation via Modbus RTU communication protocol. Omron sensors provided precise detection for product positioning and conveyor sequencing.

Over 500 I/O points were configured for comprehensive control including sensors, push buttons, limit switches, and status indicators. PLC programming implemented sequential logic for automated product flow through multiple conveyors with synchronized operation.

Control panel design incorporated all necessary components including PLCs, HMIs, AC drives, power supplies, and terminal blocks. Internal panel wiring and field wiring to conveyors was completed following industrial standards for reliability and maintainability.

Key Results & Impact

The conveyor automation systems achieved doubled workforce efficiency by automating manual material handling tasks. Significant reduction in processing time improved overall facility throughput and productivity.

Synchronized conveyor operation ensured smooth product flow through facilities, reducing bottlenecks and improving production consistency. Modbus RTU communication enabled centralized control of all conveyor drives from a single PLC system.

The comprehensive I/O configuration with 500+ points provided complete control and monitoring of conveyor operations. Automated sequencing reduced operator workload and improved safety by eliminating manual material handling in production areas.
